Step 2 of 7 - Dimension 1 of 5

Policy & Leadership

D1
Policy & Leadership
If a shareholder asked your Board today who is accountable for AI decisions at this bank - and what your AI policy says - could you give a clear, documented answer?
Why this matters: Without a named owner and a documented policy, AI governance has no foundation. For a ZSE-listed bank this is a fiduciary gap.
Discussion prompts
→ Does a board-approved AI policy exist?
→ Is accountability for AI decisions named and documented?
→ Has the Board received an AI governance briefing in the last 12 months?

Step 3 of 7 - Dimension 2 of 5

Risk Management

D2
Risk Management
Before the bank's most recent AI system or AI-powered vendor platform went live - was a structured risk assessment completed, covering bias, data quality, and regulatory compliance?
Why this matters: Banks using AI credit or fraud tools without bias assessment carry unquantified regulatory and reputational risk.
Discussion prompts
→ Was a risk assessment completed and documented before deployment?
→ Is there a register of all AI systems in use - including vendor-embedded AI?
→ Are high-risk AI applications subject to more rigorous governance than low-risk ones?
Score this dimension to continue
Step 4 of 7 - Dimension 3 of 5

Operations & Controls

D3
Operations & Controls
Do the bank's contracts with AI vendors require them to disclose model changes, notify the bank of incidents, and submit to audit - and does someone at the bank actively monitor AI outputs after deployment?
Why this matters: Most AI exposure in banks comes from third-party vendors. Without contractual accountability and monitoring, the bank carries the risk.
Discussion prompts
→ Are vendor AI contracts reviewed for governance provisions?
→ Who monitors AI system performance post-deployment - and how often?
→ Is there a current register of all third-party AI platforms in use?
Score this dimension to continue
Step 5 of 7 - Dimension 4 of 5

People & Culture

D4
People & Culture
Do customers know when AI influences a decision about them - and do staff know how to raise a concern if an AI system produces an unexpected or unfair outcome?
Why this matters: Transparency and grievance mechanisms are legal obligations under Zimbabwean law - not optional.
Discussion prompts
→ Is AI use disclosed to customers in credit or service decisions?
→ Is there a clear internal channel for staff to raise AI concerns without reprisal?
→ Are staff trained on the AI tools they use - including their limitations?
Score this dimension to continue
Step 6 of 7 - Dimension 5 of 5

Audit & Improvement

D5
Audit & Improvement
Has the bank's internal audit function ever reviewed an AI system - and does the Audit & Risk Committee receive any reporting on AI governance matters?
Why this matters: Governance that cannot be independently verified cannot be defended to regulators or shareholders.
Discussion prompts
→ Is AI governance within internal audit's current scope?
→ When did the Board or Audit Committee last discuss AI risk?
→ Is there a process to improve governance after incidents or regulatory changes?
